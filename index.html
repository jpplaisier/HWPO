------------

WALL WALKS.
- Be sure to use your warm-ups to dial in your movement here. Try to take as few steps with the hands as possible, both coming toward the wall and then away from the wall.
- Take your time to breathe at the bottom of each rep, especially if you will be fighting to finish the workout.
- The wall walks can quickly compound on each other if you move too fast, too early. Try to cycle reps from the start at a pace you could sustain, even if you are fatigued. You can always cycle these reps faster at the end of the workout if you need to!

----

ROWS.
- The perfect rowing pace will be different from athlete to athlete. Some will be able to sustain close to their 2K pace or faster, and others will need to be closer to their 5K pace or slower to start. In the ideal execution of this workout, your second row will be faster than your first row, if not EXACTLY at the same speed as the first.
- Be conservative on the first fifty calories, because there is a lot of work to come after this. If you push too hard here, you can force yourself to need to rest and move slower than you might have needed to if you paced more optimally.
- Try not to deviate much from your normal rowing technique or damper settings you would apply to an everyday metcon.
- On the last row, try to get up to speed as soon as possible and push HARD through the end!

----

DEADLIFTS.
- Aim to break these reps up in small sets with quick rests. When you break, stay over the barbell if you can.
- Know that this workout isn't won on the deadlifts, but it can be severely negatively affected if you break off too many reps to start.
- Some athletes might find success with quick singles here, especially if the goal is simply to finish the workout.

----

CLEANS.
- Most athletes will benefit from doing quick single reps here and being sure not to step back from the bar at all, if possible. Only string reps together if you are VERY confident in your barbell cycling and with the listed weight.
- If you are trying to push the pace for a top finish, try to follow the bar down between single reps to be ready to go for the next rep.

----

SNATCHES.
- Only string together reps here if you are feeling great and you are very confident with your barbell cycling at the listed weight. Again, when/if you break, be careful not to step away from the bar if you don't have to.
- Be sure you are breathing through the sets if you choose to string reps together.
- Remember that any time lost on the snatches can be gained on the remaining wall walks and the last row. Only take risks here if you are confident you will be able to hang on at a hard pace.

----

GENERAL / MISC.
- Expect this workout to hurt, especially when/if you get on for the last row! Decide before starting on how you ideally want to respond when/if difficulty sets in.
- Pay attention to your transitions between movements. With nine of them in this workout, these can add up and make what would otherwise be a good score worse!
- Have a plan for when, where, and if you will grab for the chalk bucket, and try to stick to it.
- Approach your first attempt with the intention to only hit this workout once. Of course, you can always redo the workout if you need to, but know that this particular workout can make you significantly more sore than the previous two Open workouts did. If you do end up needing to redo this one, try to space out your attempt with as much time as possible before the submission deadline.
- If you are trying to qualify to Semifinals, be sure to read through and adhere to all video submission standards at: https://games.crossfit.com/workouts/open/2025/3
- If you have time to do so, check out the member call video linked below to hear Jake D. and Josh's first thoughts over the 25.3!
