CrossFit Open 25.1

Strategy Notes

"CrossFit Open 25.1"

As many rounds and reps as possible in 15 minutes of:

3 lateral burpees over the dumbbell
3 dumbbell hang clean-to-overheads
30-foot walking lunge (2 x 15 feet)

*After completing each round, add 3 reps to the burpees and hang clean-to-overheads.

‚ôÄ 35-lb (15-kg) dumbbell
‚ôÇ 50-lb (22.5-kg) dumbbell

------------

LATERAL BURPEES OVER THE DUMBBELL.
- This is where the workout is "won" or "lost" for the majority of people. Push the pace, responsibly. Don't come out so hot that you burn out quickly, but move with some urgency here. If you are trying to finish with a good score, try to cycle these reps faster.
- Stepping up from the bottom of each rep is a great way to still move quickly but slow the rep down enough to keep moving.
- Try to alternate which foot steps up each time if you are stepping up. Consider practicing your technique in your warm-up

----

DUMBBELL HANG CLEAN TO OVERHEAD.
- If you are pushing for a big score, try your best to stay unbroken here. Alternating arms as needed is fine, but try not to alternate with every rep if you can avoid it.
- Try to remember to stay flat-footed through the dip and drive of each rep.
- If you are strong enough to maintain a push press, do so, but if you think you will need to redip for a push jerk to maintain unbroken reps, do so from the start.

----

WALKING LUNGE.
- Use this opportunity each round to move consistently and get some breaths in. Try to stay tall and keep the chest open.
- If you can maintain step-through lunges, try not to pause with the feet together between steps.
- Most athletes can plan for about five steps per each 15ft segment. When you step over the line with your last foot on the first 15ft segment, try to pivot over this same foot to switch directions and lunge back the remaining 15ft.

----

MISC.
- Moving steadily will be key here. This workout should not force most athletes to take breaks if they are pacing well, so aim to find "your pace" in your warm-up.
- Aim for your first attempt to be your only attempt. If you end up feeling like you should redo the workout after it's over, take a day or two if possible to recover, then do so, but still approach each attempt with your best effort each time.
- If you are trying to qualify to Semifinals, be sure to read through and adhere to all video submission standards at https://games.crossfit.com/workouts/open/2025.
- If you have time to do so, check out the member call video linked below to hear Mat and Josh's first thoughts over the 25.1!
